    Intelligent Plant Care System

    Project Background

    PflanziGreen is an intelligent system designed to help users manage and care for their plants more effectively. It combines photo-based plant recognition, personalized care reminders, and a visual dashboard that tracks past and upcoming plant tasks â€” offering users a friendly and reliable plant management tool.

    Context

    Plant owners often forget when they last watered or fertilized their plants, struggle to identify unknown species, or miss early warning signs like dry leaves or poor lighting. While existing apps (Planta, PictureThis, etc.) offer either identification or task tracking, few provide a seamless, all-in-one solution.
    PflanziGreen fills that gap by combining AI-powered recognition, ongoing plant care tracking, and a personalized calendar interface.

    Process

    Key Milestones:

    • Week 1: Idea brainstorming, role distribution, early concept sketches
    • Week 2 & 3: Wireframe development in Figma, color palette selection, typography decisions, and collaborative desktop layout design.
    • Week 4 & 5: Mobile-first redesign, component structuring, design refinement based on team feedback.
    • Week 6: Final submission preparation.

    Collaboration:

    • Co-designed wireframes and UI components in Figma with the other designer
    • Worked closely with the front-end developer to ensure design feasibility
    • Shared feedback loops with back-end developers for usability input

    Research

    Benchmark Analysis:
    Apps like Planta, PictureThis, and PlantIn provide valuable features, but most specialize in either identification or care reminders, not both.

    Notable References:

    • PlantIn (Industry): AI-based identification + contextual care suggestions (weather-aware).
    • Flora Incognita (Research): Deep learning model for accurate wild plant classification.

    Design Goals

    The design goals were as follows:

    • Deliver a clean, intuitive interface for plant care, even for novice users.
    • Create a central dashboard that highlights important tasks visually.
    • Ensure a consistent and developer-friendly component structure.

    Design Problems

    • Initial misunderstanding of the projects format (mobile app vs. responsive web).
    • Potential complexity in components that could delay development.
    • Lack of a design system at the start caused slower progress and inconsistencies.

    Design Decisions

    To address the identified challenges, several decisions were made:

    • Shifted from mobile app to mobile-first responsive web design after project clarification.
    • Built modular, reusable UI components in Figma.
    • Established a soft, nature-inspired color palette to reflect the plant theme.
    • Ensured feasibility through regular communication with the dev team.

    Reflections

    What I learned:

    • Designing with implementation in mind.
    • Communicating visual ideas clearly and adapting quickly to shifting project scopes
    • Managing components efficiently in Figma.

    What I would do differently:

    • Align on target platform from the start to avoid mismatches.
    • Organize a kickoff workshop to define shared vision, goals, and user expectations.
    • Build a basic design system early for better speed and consistency.
    • Involve front-end developers at the wireframe stage to ensure feasibility.
